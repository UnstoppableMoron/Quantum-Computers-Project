Introduction to Quantum Computing

Classical vs. Quantum Computing

A typical computer relies on binary bits, which can only be represented by zeroes and ones, a quantum computer instead uses qubits, which can be both simultaneously. This allows for much more complex calculations and analytics that would be impossible for a classical computer to run.

General Uses

Quantum computing is used for artificial intelligence, data analytics, cryptography, etc. It is also capable of solving complex problems in a fraction of the time it would take a regular supercomputer.
